# CASE_STUDY_SYSTEM

## Philosophy

This portfolio is not a collection of projects.

It is a collection of design stories.

Every case study should communicate:

* Thinking
* Process
* Decision-making
* Outcomes
* Craft

The goal is not to show screens.

The goal is to demonstrate how problems were understood and solved.

Visitors should leave with confidence in the designer's ability to think strategically and execute with precision.

---

# What We Are Optimizing For

Not:

"That looks nice."

But:

"I would trust this person to design a product."

Every section should reinforce credibility.

Every image should support the narrative.

Every paragraph should have a purpose.

---

# Case Study Structure

Every project follows the same framework.

This creates consistency across the portfolio.

## 01 Hero

The first screen should answer:

What is this?

Who is it for?

Why does it matter?

Include:

* Project title
* Short description
* Role
* Timeline
* Company or concept
* Platform

Visual:

Large product imagery or video.

Avoid showing multiple competing visuals.

One strong visual is enough.

---

## 02 Context

Introduce the world.

What industry exists around this project?

What situation created the opportunity?

What were users experiencing?

Avoid:

"We wanted to create..."

Focus on reality.

Explain the environment before the solution.

Visual:

Research images
Industry visuals
Supporting diagrams

---

## 03 Challenge

What problem existed?

Why was it important?

What was broken?

What friction existed?

What impact did the problem create?

This section should make readers care.

Without a clear challenge, the solution has no value.

Visual:

Journey maps
Current state diagrams
Pain point illustrations

---

## 04 Insight

This is often the most important section.

What did you learn?

What shifted your perspective?

What changed the direction of the work?

Strong designers identify insights.

Weak designers list observations.

Example:

Observation:

"Users struggled with reports."

Insight:

"Users didn't need more reports. They needed help understanding what the reports meant."

Visual:

Framework diagrams
Insight models
Mental models

---

## 05 Strategy

Before showing screens:

Explain the approach.

What principles guided decisions?

What constraints shaped the solution?

What tradeoffs were made?

This section demonstrates product thinking.

Visual:

Design principles
Strategic frameworks
Prioritization diagrams

---

## 06 Solution

Break the solution into meaningful chapters.

Never dump all screens at once.

Each chapter should follow:

Problem

↓

Design Decision

↓

Outcome

Example:

Sales Tracking

Business Insights

AI Coach

Business Health

Each chapter should feel self-contained.

Visual:

Screens
Flows
Interactions
Videos

Always explain why a design decision exists.

Never show UI without context.

---

## 07 System Thinking

Show how the product scales.

Possible examples:

* Design system
* Component strategy
* Information architecture
* Content strategy
* Interaction patterns

Keep this concise.

The purpose is to demonstrate rigor.

Not to display every component created.

Visual:

Architecture diagrams
System visuals
Component previews

---

## 08 Outcome

What changed?

What impact was created?

Use real metrics when available.

If metrics do not exist:

Discuss intended outcomes.

Examples:

* Reduced complexity
* Increased understanding
* Faster completion
* Better decision making
* Increased confidence

Avoid inventing numbers.

Never use fake metrics.

---

## 09 Reflection

Every case study ends with reflection.

This is where seniority becomes visible.

Discuss:

* What worked
* What was difficult
* What surprised you
* What you would improve

Avoid:

"Overall the project was successful."

Be specific.

Show growth.

---

# Visual Rhythm

Every case study should alternate between:

Text

↓

Visual

↓

Text

↓

Visual

↓

Text

↓

Visual

Avoid large walls of text.

Avoid image galleries.

The story should flow naturally.

---

# Image Rules

Every image must answer one question:

"What does this help explain?"

If the answer is unclear:

Remove it.

Avoid decorative imagery.

Avoid filler screenshots.

Every visual should advance the story.

---

# Video Rules

Video should be used sparingly.

Use video when:

* Motion matters
* Interactions matter
* Workflows matter

Do not use video simply because it looks impressive.

Recommended:

1 Hero Video Maximum

1–3 Interaction Videos Maximum

Per case study.

---

# Writing Style

Write like a product designer.

Not like a student.

Avoid:

"We decided to..."

"We wanted to..."

"I thought it would be nice..."

Prefer:

"Research revealed..."

"This created..."

"To address this..."

"This led to..."

The tone should be thoughtful and confident.

---

# SPAL-Specific Storytelling Rule

For SPAL:

The story is not about bookkeeping.

The story is about understanding.

The central narrative should always return to:

Information → Understanding → Better Decisions

This is the core belief behind the product.

Every section should reinforce this idea.

---

# Final Review Checklist

Before publishing any case study ask:

1. Does the problem feel real?
2. Is there a clear insight?
3. Does every visual support the story?
4. Does the solution feel inevitable?
5. Does the reflection demonstrate growth?
6. Would this feel at home in a premium design publication?

If any answer is no:

Refine before publishing.
